Ventilation — mask vs Llume on dual axes

Ve — ventilation

Llume Ve is pinned at ~40 L/min throughout (saturated). Mask Ve rises and falls with each interval from ~68 on-work up to ~86 on-recovery.

Tv — tidal volume

Mask Tv stays around 3.3 L; Llume Tv around 1.7 L. Different magnitudes, similar per-cycle behavior.

Rf — respiration rate

Both sensors read Rf in the same ~20–30 bpm range and track each other closely.

Ve — ventilation

Dual axes: mask on left (68–87 L/min), Llume on right (39–42 L/min). With each sensor on its own scale, the work-vs-rest shape of the mask is clearly visible while the Llume flatness stands out — Llume is pinned at its saturation ceiling regardless of power.

Rf — respiration rate

Bars are nearly equal height at every power — the two sensors agree on breathing frequency across the whole range.

Tv — tidal volume

Dual axes: each sensor has its own scale (mask ~3 L, Llume ~1.7 L). Shape comparison at a glance.

Tv / 3.2 — as % of reference

Mask tidal volume expressed as a percentage of 3.2 L. Values near 100% mean he’s breathing close to the reference depth across both work and rest.
